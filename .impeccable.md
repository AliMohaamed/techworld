## Design Context

### Users

**Storefront**: Egyptian consumers (18-45) browsing and buying electronics/gadgets via social commerce (WhatsApp-driven COD). 90%+ on mobile, often on slower connections, making impulsive flash-sale purchases. They need speed, clarity, and trust — not complexity. Arabic is the primary language, English is secondary.

**Admin**: Internal operations staff (verification agents, shipping coordinators, super admins) managing orders, inventory, and fraud prevention in long shifts. They need data density, immediate actionability, and visual hierarchy to process queues quickly. Arabic and English both supported.

### Brand Personality

**Professional, Premium, Efficient** — TechWorld feels like a trustworthy electronics retailer you'd recommend to a friend, not a garage sale. Every interaction should feel deliberate and polished. Urgency comes from clear stock indicators and strong CTAs, not from visual noise. The gold brand color carries authority and warmth.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone**: Warm premium retail — think Noon.com's clean product-forward layouts combined with the confidence of a high-end electronics brand. Dark backgrounds with gold accents for product showcase impact; light surfaces for readability. The current dark-warm palette (`#1a1814` background, `#ffc105` gold) is strong and distinctive.

**References**: Noon.com, Jumia — familiar to Egyptian shoppers, product-forward, clean grid layouts, strong pricing visibility, clear stock urgency indicators.

**Anti-references**: No dark neon gamer aesthetic (RGB glow, cyberpunk). No corporate SaaS dashboard minimalism (Stripe/Linear coldness on the storefront). No red/purple gradients that scream AI-generated.

**Theme**: System preference — respect the user's OS setting for light/dark. Both themes must be fully designed, not an afterthought. Dark mode should feel warm and rich, not cold gray.

**Typography**:
- Storefront English: Space Grotesk (display/headings) — geometric, techy, distinctive
- Admin English: Inter — proven for data-dense UIs, excellent readability at small sizes
- Both Arabic: Cairo — clean, modern Arabic with good weight range

**Color**: The gold `#ffc105` is the brand anchor. In dark mode, warm darks (`#1a1814`) keep surfaces from feeling cold. In light mode, white surfaces with gold accents create a premium feel. Avoid purple-blue gradients, neon cyan, and AI-slop palettes. Expand the palette with warm neutrals tinted toward the brand hue.

### Design Principles

1. **Mobile-thumbable**: Every touch target >= 44px. Critical actions (Add to Cart, Confirm Payment) must be reachable with the thumb at the bottom of the screen. 90%+ mobile traffic means mobile IS the primary experience.

2. **Professional confidence**: No gimmicks or visual tricks. Trust is built through consistency, clarity, and restraint. The gold accent earns its impact because it's used sparingly — on CTAs, active states, and key highlights only.

3. **Efficiency over decoration**: Every element must earn its place. Flash sale urgency comes from stock indicators and countdown logic, not from animating badges or pulsing colors. The admin dashboard prioritizes data density and immediate actionability.

4. **Bilingual harmony**: Arabic (RTL) and English (LTR) are first-class citizens. Layouts, spacing, and component sizing must work equally well in both directions. Use `ltr:` / `rtl:` Tailwind variants. Arabic Cairo and English Space Grotesk/Inter must feel visually balanced side by side.

5. **Warmth at every surface**: Neutrals are tinted toward the brand's warm gold hue (OKLCH hue ~85). Avoid pure cold grays — they create visual dissonance with the warm brand color. Dark mode surfaces use `#1a1814`, not pure `#111`.
